# Trade Industry Archetypes Research

## Source: Build Perspectives - 4 Main Construction Industry Archetypes

### 1. ARCHITECTS/DESIGNERS
**Key Motivations:**
- Want to drive by a project and feel good about it
- Want to show off their work
- Don't want to worry about low-quality materials
- Care about aesthetics and design integrity

**Sub-personas:**
- Principal Architects (strategic, reputation-focused)
- Designers (detail-oriented, creative)

**What Appeals:**
- Quality materials
- Design flexibility
- Professional presentation
- Portfolio-worthy results

---

### 2. DEVELOPERS/OWNERS
**Key Motivations:**
- Have investors to answer to
- Responsible for budget and timeline
- Only 20% of commercial projects are on-time and in-budget
- Must make capital calls when projects go over

**Characteristics:**
- Most at stake in construction projects
- Used to getting their way
- Can be abrasive and demanding
- Looking for someone to push back on their demands (test of competence)
- Value reliability and efficiency

**What Appeals:**
- Cost transparency
- Timeline reliability
- Professional pushback with solid reasoning
- Efficiency in processes

---

### 3. GENERAL CONTRACTORS
**Key Motivations:**
- Value time and cost efficiency the most
- Managing labor, materials, and client relationships
- Want to add value for repeat business
- Focus on value engineering

**Characteristics:**
- Have their own customer avatars (developers)
- Think about raising problems to create value
- Balance quality with cost

**Two Types:**
- Mastery of Labor (70%)
- Mastery of Materials (30%)

**What Appeals:**
- Time savings
- Cost efficiency
- Tools that help manage labor/materials
- Value engineering opportunities

---

### 4. INSTALLERS/SUBCONTRACTORS
**Key Motivations:**
- 70% mastery of labor people
- 30% mastery of materials people
- Focus on efficient installation
- Channel considerations (direct vs one-step)

**What Appeals:**
- Clear specifications
- Efficient processes
- Materials that help with projects
- Time-saving tools

---

## Key Insight: Ideal Customer Avatar (ICA) Approach
From Marie Forleo's B-School methodology:
1. Compile traits all ideal customers have in common
2. Create demographic profile (age, gender, education, income, location)
3. Write marketing copy as if speaking directly to that person
4. Spend marketing dollars attracting that specific persona

## Critical Finding
"It's imperative to view the building construction industry as PEOPLE, not companies"
- Empathy is the #1 way to earn more sales
- Understanding specific concerns, problems, and everyday issues is key

## Source: Unmudl - Personality Traits Best Suited for Trades

### KEY PERSONALITY TRAITS FOR TRADESPEOPLE

#### 1. CONSCIENTIOUSNESS - The Bedrock of Professionalism
- Responsible, thorough, organized, diligent
- Key predictor of professional success
- Pay attention to detail, follow safety protocols, meet deadlines
- **Trade Fit:** Electricians, machinists, maintenance technicians, carpenters, plumbers, HVAC

#### 2. PROBLEM-SOLVING & ADAPTABILITY - Thriving in the Unexpected
- Trade jobs are rarely predictable
- Must think on feet without immediate supervision
- Stay calm when things don't go according to plan
- Quick thinking prevents shutdowns and disasters
- **Trade Fit:** Welders, powerline installers, plumbers, HVAC technicians

#### 3. PHYSICAL & MENTAL RESILIENCE - Grit Under Pressure
- Long hours, heavy lifting, extreme conditions
- Mental resilience for handling job stress, setbacks, client demands
- Push through fatigue, maintain focus during repetitive tasks
- **Trade Fit:** Roofers, ironworkers, powerline installers, wind turbine technicians

#### 4. PRACTICAL INTELLIGENCE - Hands-On Thinking
- "Street smarts" or "mechanical aptitude"
- Quickly understand complex systems, visualize solutions
- Manipulate tools effectively
- **Trade Fit:** Machinists, air compressor technicians, electricians, carpenters

#### 5. ANALYTICAL THINKING & RATIONALITY - The Logical Edge
- Dissect complex issues, evaluate options
- Make sound decisions under pressure
- **Trade Fit:** All trades requiring diagnostics and troubleshooting

#### 6. DETAIL-ORIENTATION - Precision Matters
- Electricians: detail-oriented, emotionally stable, curious
- Cautious approach to safety-critical work
- **Trade Fit:** Electricians especially

#### 7. INTERPERSONAL SKILLS - Client-Facing Work
- HVAC technicians, plumbers, electricians work in customer homes
- Require tact and professionalism
- Direct client interactions are common

## KEY UX IMPLICATIONS FOR VALIDT

### For Tradespeople:
1. **Clear, precise information** - Appeals to conscientiousness
2. **Problem-solving tools** - Help them think on their feet
3. **Resilient/reliable platform** - Matches their work ethic
4. **Practical, hands-on interface** - No unnecessary complexity
5. **Logical, analytical data presentation** - Supports decision-making

### For Blue-Collar App Design (from research):
1. **Large, clear buttons** - Easy to tap with work gloves
2. **Simple navigation** - No time for complex menus
3. **Offline functionality** - Job sites may lack connectivity
4. **Multilingual support** - Diverse workforce
5. **Quick onboarding** - Get to work fast
