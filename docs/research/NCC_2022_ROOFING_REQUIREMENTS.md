## Part 7.1 - Scope and Application

### 7.1.1 Scope (New for 2022)
This Section sets out the Deemed-to-Satisfy Provisions for:
1. Metal sheet roofing (Part 7.2)
2. Roof tiles (Part 7.3)
3. Gutters and downpipes (Part 7.4)
4. Timber and composite wall cladding (Part 7.5)

**Key Note:** Other construction methods may be used to achieve the same results provided they comply with the appropriate Performance Requirements.

## Part 7.2 - Sheet Roofing

### 7.2.2 Corrosion Protection and Compatibility
**Citation:** NCC 2022, Part 7.2.2

1. Metal sheet roofing must be protected from corrosion in accordance with Table 7.2.2a
2. Different metals in a roofing system must be compatible (Tables 7.2.2b-e)
3. **Prohibited combinations:**
   - No lead materials upstream from aluminium/zinc coated materials
   - No lead materials on drinking water catchment roofs
   - No copper materials upstream from galvanized coated materials

### 7.2.3 Minimum Pitches
**Citation:** NCC 2022, Part 7.2.3
- Metal sheet roofing must comply with minimum pitch requirements per Figure 7.2.3

### 7.2.4 Maximum Spans
**Citation:** NCC 2022, Part 7.2.4
- Must comply with maximum span between roofing supports per Table 7.2.4 and Figure 7.2.4

### 7.2.5 Fixing of Metal Sheet Roofing
**Citation:** NCC 2022, Part 7.2.5

Metal sheet roofing must:
1. Be fixed through roofing (crest fastening) or have concealed fasteners
2. Be fixed at spacings per Table 7.2.5
3. Use fixings of compatible metal (Tables 7.2.2b-e)
4. Employ anti-capillary feature in side lap when using both clipped and pierced fastening

### 7.2.6 Installation of Roofing Sheets
**Citation:** NCC 2022, Part 7.2.6

Sheets must be:
1. Laid using complete lengths from fascia to ridge where possible
2. Where complete length cannot be laid:
   - Each run laid bottom to top before moving to next run
   - Minimum end lap:
     - Above 15° (1:4): 150mm
     - 5-15° (1:12-1:4): 200mm
3. Stop ended (turned up 60°) at ridge line

### 7.2.7 Flashings and Cappings
**Citation:** NCC 2022, Part 7.2.7

Requirements:
1. Must be purpose made, machine-folded sheet metal sections
2. Compatible with upstream/downstream materials
3. Joints minimum 75mm, lapped in direction of fall
4. Wall/step flashings fastened into masonry at max 500mm intervals
5. Lead flashings prohibited with prepainted steel or zinc/aluminium steel
6. Anti-capillary breaks required:
   - Flat surfaces: 10mm/30° fold
   - Other surfaces: 10mm/90° or 135° fold

### 7.2.8 Water Discharge
**Citation:** NCC 2022, Part 7.2.8
- Sheets must overhang fascia by minimum 50mm where eaves gutter provided
