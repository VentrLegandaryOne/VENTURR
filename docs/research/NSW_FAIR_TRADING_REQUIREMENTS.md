# NSW Fair Trading - Licensing and Contract Requirements

## Source Information
- **Authority:** NSW Government / Building Commission NSW / Fair Trading
- **Retrieved:** December 2024

---

## Contractor Licensing Requirements

### Licence Threshold
**$5,000 Rule:** A contractor licence is required to carry out, advertise, or contract for residential building work in NSW valued at more than $5,000 in labour and materials (including GST).

### Roofing-Related Licence Categories

| Category | Work Type | Licence Required |

| Roof Plumbing | Gutters, downpipes, flashings | Yes - Trade Work |
| Roof Tiling | Tile installation/repair | Yes - Trade Work |

| Metal Fabrication | Custom metalwork | Yes - Trade Work |
| General Building | All residential building work | Yes - Contractor |

**Source:** https://www.nsw.gov.au/business-and-economy/licences-and-credentials/building-and-trade-licences-and-registrations/categories-of-work

## Contract Requirements

### Written Contract Mandatory
Builders and tradespeople must provide a written contract for residential building work when:
- Contract price exceeds $5,000 (including GST), OR
- Price unknown but reasonable market cost of labour and materials exceeds $5,000

### Contract Size Categories

| Contract Value | Category | Requirements |
|----------------|----------|--------------|
| $5,000 - $20,000 | Small Works | Basic written contract required |
| Over $20,000 | Large Jobs | Full contract with additional requirements |

### Large Jobs Contract Requirements (Over $20,000)
Must include:
- Insurance details
- Progress payments schedule
- Warranties
- 5-day cooling-off period
- Home Building Compensation (HBC) insurance certificate

### Deposit Limits
**Maximum deposit: 10% of contract price**
- Applies to both small and large jobs
- Cannot request more than 10% upfront

**Source:** https://www.nsw.gov.au/housing-and-construction/compliance-and-regulation/your-obligations-to-your-customers/guide-to-providing-home-building-contracts

## Consumer Building Guide
For work exceeding $5,000, contractors must provide homeowners with a copy of the Consumer Building Guide.

---

## Home Building Compensation (HBC) Insurance

### When Required
- Residential building work over $20,000
- Must be obtained before work commences
- Certificate must be provided to homeowner

### Coverage
Protects homeowners if:
- Builder dies, disappears, or becomes insolvent
- Defective or incomplete work

## Verification Implications for VALIDT

### Must Check in Quotes:

1. **Licence Number Verification**
   - Quote should display contractor licence number
   - Can be verified via NSW Fair Trading licence check

2. **Contract Compliance**
   - Quotes over $5,000 should reference written contract
   - Quotes over $20,000 should mention HBC insurance

3. **Deposit Compliance**
   - Deposit requested should not exceed 10%
   - Flag if deposit exceeds this threshold

4. **Required Inclusions**
   - Labour and materials breakdown
   - GST clearly stated
   - Start and completion dates
   - Payment schedule

### Red Flags to Identify:
- No licence number displayed
- Deposit request exceeding 10%
- No mention of written contract for work >$5,000
- No HBC insurance reference for work >$20,000
- Vague pricing without GST breakdown
