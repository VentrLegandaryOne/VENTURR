## Frequently Asked Questions

### Calculator Usage

**Q: Why are my results higher than my usual estimates?**
A: The calculator includes all mandatory on-costs (superannuation, WorkCover, insurance, PPE) that many contractors underestimate or forget. It also uses realistic installation times based on industry data. Review the crew breakdown to see the detailed cost structure.

**Q: Can I adjust the calculator settings to match my actual costs?**
A: The calculator uses industry-standard rates and multipliers. If your actual costs differ significantly, you may need to adjust your profit margin or review your crew efficiency. The calculator is designed to ensure you're not underpricing.

**Q: How accurate are the weather delay buffers?**
A: The buffers are based on historical weather data for Australian regions. They represent realistic contingencies, not worst-case scenarios. You can adjust the season selection if you have specific weather information for your project timing.

### Crew Selection

**Q: When should I use a specialized crew instead of a standard crew?**
A: Use specialized crews when their specific capabilities match the project requirements. Re-Roofing Specialist for any project with removal, Emergency Response for urgent work, Heritage & Custom for heritage buildings, Commercial Large-Scale for large commercial projects. The specialized crews provide better outcomes and efficiency for their target project types.

**Q: Can I use a larger crew to speed up a project?**
A: Yes, larger crews complete work faster due to higher efficiency multipliers. However, they also cost more per hour. Use the calculator to compare different crew options for the same project to find the best balance of cost and timeline.

**Q: What if I don't have the exact crew composition shown in the calculator?**
A: The crew compositions represent optimal configurations. If your actual crew differs, choose the closest match based on total skill level and crew size. The calculator provides a benchmark for what the project should cost with an optimal crew.

### Pricing and Quoting

**Q: Should I show clients the detailed crew breakdown?**
A: This depends on your market and client type. The detailed breakdown demonstrates transparency and justifies your pricing, which many clients appreciate. However, some contractors prefer to show only the total labor cost. The calculator provides both options.

**Q: How do I handle clients who say my quote is too high?**
A: Use the calculator's detailed breakdowns to explain your pricing. Show the mandatory on-costs, material costs, and realistic labor hours. Explain how your pricing ensures quality work and proper business practices. If necessary, adjust the profit margin or crew selection, but don't eliminate mandatory on-costs.

**Q: Can I use the calculator for fixed-price contracts?**
A: Yes, the calculator is designed for fixed-price quoting. The weather delay buffers and complexity settings help ensure your fixed price covers the actual project costs. For commercial fixed-price contracts, consider adding a contingency line item for unforeseen conditions.

### Technical Questions

**Q: How are the labor rates calculated?**
A: Labor rates are based on 2025 PayScale and Indeed Australia data for roofing tradespeople, adjusted for skill level and region. The calculator includes all mandatory on-costs (superannuation 12%, WorkCover, public liability, PPE) and optional on-costs (tools, vehicles, administration) for comprehensive accuracy.

**Q: Why does tile roofing take so much longer than metal?**
A: Tile roofing requires individual tile placement, is much heavier (affecting handling speed), requires more precise alignment, and involves more complex flashing and ridge work. The 2.0-2.5x multiplier for tile is based on actual industry installation times.

**Q: How are weather delay buffers calculated?**
A: Weather buffers are based on historical weather data for Australian regions, considering rain days, temperature impacts on work, and daylight hours by season. Winter's 25% buffer reflects the significantly higher risk of weather delays during that season.
