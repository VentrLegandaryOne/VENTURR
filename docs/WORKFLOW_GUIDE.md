## Workflow Overview

Every Venturr project follows a consistent workflow that ensures accuracy and professionalism. The standard workflow consists of five key stages that guide you from initial client contact through to quote delivery and project commencement.

### Standard Venturr Workflow

The workflow begins with **Project Creation**, where you establish the project in the system and capture essential client information. This is followed by **Site Measurement**, either through physical site visits or plan analysis, where you gather accurate roof dimensions and characteristics. Next comes **Labor Calculation**, where the Enhanced Labor Calculator processes your measurements and project parameters to generate precise cost estimates. The **Quote Generation** stage then transforms these calculations into professional, client-ready quotations. Finally, **Quote Delivery** completes the cycle, sending the quote to the client and tracking their response.

This structured approach ensures that every project receives the same level of professional attention, while the system's flexibility allows you to adapt to specific project requirements. The following workflows demonstrate how this standard process applies to different project types, highlighting the unique considerations and settings for each scenario.

## Workflow 1: New Residential Construction

This workflow covers standard new construction projects, which represent the most common use case for roofing contractors. These projects benefit from clean working conditions and straightforward installation without the complications of removal or structural concerns.

### Project Characteristics

New residential construction projects typically involve simple to moderate roof complexity on homes ranging from 120 to 250 square meters. The work environment is clean, with no existing roof removal required, and coordination with other building trades is essential. Standard timelines range from one to two weeks, depending on roof size and complexity.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

**Step 1: Initial Client Contact and Project Setup**

When a builder or homeowner contacts you about a new construction project, begin by gathering essential information. You need the property address, builder contact details, and approximate roof area. Request architectural plans if available, as these provide valuable detail for accurate estimation. Confirm the expected construction timeline and any specific material preferences the client may have.

Create the project in Venturr by navigating to the dashboard and clicking "New Project." Enter the project title using a clear format such as "New Construction - [Address]." Select "Residential" as the property type and input the complete property address. Add client information including the builder's name, email, and phone number. Set the project status to "Estimating" to track its progress through your workflow.

**Step 2: Plan Review and Measurement**

If architectural plans are available, review them carefully to identify the roof type, which is typically gable or hip for residential construction. Measure the roof dimensions, noting the length and width of each roof plane. Count the number of valleys and hips, as these affect installation complexity. Measure or calculate the roof pitch, usually specified in the plans. Identify any penetrations such as skylights, vents, or chimneys that will require flashing work.

For projects without plans, conduct a site visit to take physical measurements. Use a laser measure for accuracy and safety. Take photographs of the roof structure to document conditions and assist with later planning. Note any access challenges that might affect crew efficiency or safety requirements.

**Step 3: Labor Calculator Configuration**

Open the Labor Calculator from the project page and navigate to the Dimensions tab. Enter the measured roof length and width. Select the appropriate roof type based on your observations. Input the roof pitch in degrees. Choose the roofing material, typically Colorbond or Zincalume for new construction. Set the waste percentage, using ten percent for simple roofs and fifteen percent for more complex designs.

Switch to the Labor tab to configure project-specific settings. Set the roof complexity based on the number of roof planes and features. The pitch category should auto-populate based on your entered pitch angle. Enter the count of valleys and hips. Select the crew composition, typically Standard Crew for most residential projects or Enhanced Crew if faster completion is required. Choose your region for accurate cost-of-living adjustments. Under Advanced Factors, set the material type to Colorbond or Metal. Confirm the removal type is set to "None" for new construction. Select the current season to apply appropriate weather delay buffers. Enable optional on-costs to ensure comprehensive cost coverage.

Move to the Pricing tab and set your profit margin, typically twenty to twenty-five percent for standard residential work. Enable GST inclusion for the final quote.

**Step 4: Calculate and Review Results**

Click "Calculate Takeoff" to generate comprehensive results. Review the roof details section to verify that calculated areas match your expectations. Examine the labor details, paying particular attention to total days required and labor cost per square meter. Check the cost breakdown to ensure materials and labor costs align with market rates. Note the grand total and per-square-meter cost for comparison with your typical pricing.

Review the crew breakdown to understand the detailed cost structure. Verify that all mandatory on-costs are included and that optional on-costs are appropriate for the project. If results seem inconsistent with expectations, adjust complexity settings, crew selection, or other parameters as needed.

**Step 5: Generate Professional Quote**

Navigate to the Quote Generator from the project page. The system should automatically import your labor calculation data. Review the pre-populated line items for accuracy. Add any additional items not covered by the calculator, such as gutter replacement, downpipe installation, or fascia repairs. Adjust descriptions to match your company's standard terminology. Set payment terms, typically a deposit of thirty to fifty percent with the balance due on completion. Add project-specific terms and conditions, including warranty information and project timeline.

Review the quote preview to ensure professional presentation. Check that all costs are clearly itemized and that the total matches your calculation. Verify that company branding and contact information are correct.

**Step 6: Quote Delivery and Follow-Up**

Send the quote to the client via email directly from Venturr. Include a professional cover message explaining the quote structure and highlighting key benefits. Set a follow-up reminder for three to five business days. Save a PDF copy for your records.

Follow up with the client after the specified period. Be prepared to answer questions about pricing, timeline, or materials. If requested, adjust the quote based on client feedback and regenerate. Once accepted, convert the project status to "Scheduled" and begin planning the work.

### Key Settings for This Workflow

For new residential construction, configure the calculator with these typical settings: Complexity is usually Simple or Standard, depending on roof features. Material type should be Colorbond or Metal for standard installations. The removal type must be set to None. Crew selection is typically Standard Crew for most projects, or Enhanced Crew for larger homes or faster timelines. Season should match the expected construction period. Optional on-costs should be enabled for accurate total cost estimation.

### Expected Results

A typical 150-square-meter new construction project with standard complexity should yield eight to ten days total duration, including weather buffer. Labor costs typically range from six thousand to eight thousand dollars, depending on region and crew selection. Material costs fall between five thousand and seven thousand dollars for standard Colorbond. The grand total usually ranges from fifteen thousand to twenty thousand dollars, including GST. Cost per square meter typically falls between one hundred and one hundred thirty dollars.

## Workflow 2: Tile to Metal Re-Roofing

Re-roofing projects involve removing an existing roof and installing a new one. These projects are more complex than new construction and require specialized crew selection and careful planning for removal, disposal, and potential structural issues.

### Project Characteristics

Tile to metal re-roofing projects typically involve existing homes with terracotta or concrete tile roofs being converted to modern Colorbond metal roofing. The roof area usually ranges from 120 to 300 square meters. These projects require old roof removal and disposal, potential batten replacement, structural assessment for hidden issues, and occupant protection during the work. Timelines typically extend from two to three weeks, accounting for removal time and weather delays.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

**Step 1: Initial Assessment and Client Consultation**

When a homeowner contacts you about re-roofing, schedule a thorough site inspection. During the inspection, assess the existing roof condition, identifying the tile type (terracotta or concrete), checking for asbestos in older tiles (pre-1990s), noting any structural sagging or damage, and examining the condition of existing battens and sarking. Take comprehensive photographs documenting the current roof condition, any damage or deterioration, access points for removal, and areas requiring special attention.

Discuss with the homeowner their goals for the re-roofing project, preferred new roofing material and color, timeline expectations and any deadlines, budget constraints or financing needs, and concerns about noise, dust, and disruption during the work.

Create the project in Venturr with a descriptive title such as "Re-Roofing - Tile to Metal - [Address]." Set the property type to Residential and input complete property and client information. Set the project status to "Assessment" initially, then move to "Estimating" after the site visit.

**Step 2: Detailed Measurement and Documentation**

Measure the existing roof carefully, as tile roofs may have different dimensions than the planned metal roof. Record the length and width of each roof plane. Count valleys, hips, and ridges accurately. Measure the roof pitch, which may differ from plans if the house is older. Note all penetrations including skylights, vents, chimneys, and satellite dishes. Document any structural concerns that may require additional work.

Assess removal complexity by examining tile attachment methods, checking for multiple layers of roofing, identifying asbestos presence (requires licensed removal), and evaluating access for waste disposal. Estimate the volume of waste for disposal planning, as tile roofs generate significant waste volume.

**Step 3: Labor Calculator Configuration for Re-Roofing**

Open the Labor Calculator and configure the Dimensions tab with measured roof dimensions. Select the roof type based on the existing structure. Input the measured roof pitch. Choose the new roofing material, typically Colorbond for tile conversions. Set waste percentage to fifteen to twenty percent for re-roofing projects, as they typically involve more cuts and adjustments.

In the Labor tab, set roof complexity, typically Standard or Complex for tile re-roofing due to potential structural issues. The pitch category should reflect the measured pitch. Enter the accurate count of valleys and hips. Select "Re-Roofing Specialist" as the crew composition for optimal efficiency. This crew includes a removal specialist and provides thirty percent faster removal times. Choose your region for cost adjustments.

Under Advanced Factors, set the material type to Colorbond or Metal for the new roof. Set the removal type to match the existing roof: Terracotta Tile (adds 0.25 hours per square meter) or Concrete Tile (adds 0.30 hours per square meter). If asbestos is present, select Asbestos (Licensed) which adds 0.65 hours per square meter and requires specialist contractors. Select the season based on expected project timing, noting that winter re-roofing projects face significant weather delays. Enable optional on-costs for comprehensive cost coverage.

In the Pricing tab, set your profit margin, typically twenty-five to thirty percent for re-roofing due to higher complexity. Enable GST inclusion.

**Step 4: Calculate and Analyze Results**

Click "Calculate Takeoff" and carefully review the comprehensive results. Pay special attention to the installation hours for the new roof, removal hours (which can be thirty to fifty percent of total time), total hours combining installation and removal, base days required, weather delay buffer (especially important for winter projects), and total days with weather buffer.

Examine the cost breakdown, noting materials cost for the new roof, labor cost including both installation and removal, and the grand total with profit and GST. Review the crew breakdown to understand how the Re-Roofing Specialist crew composition affects costs and timeline. Verify that removal time is properly included and that weather delays are realistic for the season.

**Step 5: Additional Cost Items**

Beyond the calculator results, consider additional costs that may apply to re-roofing projects. Waste disposal can cost one thousand to three thousand dollars depending on volume and local disposal fees. Batten replacement may be necessary if existing battens are damaged or inadequate, typically adding two thousand to five thousand dollars. Structural repairs might be required for sagging or damaged roof structures, with costs varying by extent of damage. Asbestos removal requires licensed contractors if present, adding significant cost (five thousand to fifteen thousand dollars). Temporary weatherproofing may be needed if the project spans multiple days, and scaffolding or safety equipment might be required for steep or high roofs.

Add these items to your quote as separate line items with clear descriptions and individual pricing.

**Step 6: Generate Comprehensive Quote**

Navigate to the Quote Generator and import the labor calculation. Add all additional cost items identified in your assessment. Create detailed line item descriptions that clearly separate removal costs, new roof installation, waste disposal, and any structural or batten work. Set payment terms appropriate for re-roofing, typically thirty percent deposit, thirty percent on removal completion, and forty percent on final completion. Include comprehensive terms and conditions covering disruption management, waste disposal methods, structural discovery clauses, and weather delay policies.

Review the complete quote to ensure all costs are clearly itemized and justified. Verify that the timeline is realistic, accounting for removal, disposal, and installation. Check that warranty information is clear and comprehensive.

**Step 7: Client Presentation and Project Planning**

Present the quote to the client in person when possible for re-roofing projects. Walk through the quote section by section, explaining removal process and timeline, new roof specifications and benefits, additional work items and their necessity, total cost breakdown and payment schedule, and expected project duration and weather considerations.

Address client concerns about disruption, noise, and dust protection, waste disposal and site cleanliness, protection of landscaping and property, and communication during the project. Discuss the discovery clause for hidden structural issues that may be revealed during removal.

Once the quote is accepted, plan the project carefully. Schedule the Re-Roofing Specialist crew, arrange waste disposal (skip bins or truck removal), order materials with appropriate lead time, notify neighbors of upcoming work, and prepare site protection measures.

### Key Settings for This Workflow

Configure the calculator specifically for re-roofing with these settings: Complexity is typically Standard or Complex due to removal and potential issues. Material type should be set to Colorbond or Metal for the new roof. Removal type must match the existing roof (Terracotta Tile, Concrete Tile, or Asbestos if applicable). Crew selection should be Re-Roofing Specialist for optimal efficiency and removal expertise. Season selection is critical, as weather delays significantly impact re-roofing projects. Optional on-costs should be enabled, and additional items for disposal, batten replacement, and structural work must be added separately.

### Expected Results

A typical 180-square-meter tile to metal re-roofing project should yield these results: Total duration of fourteen to twenty days, including removal, disposal, and installation with weather buffer. Installation hours of seventy to ninety hours for the new roof. Removal hours of forty-five to fifty-five hours for tile removal. Labor costs of twelve thousand to sixteen thousand dollars, including both installation and removal. Material costs of six thousand to eight thousand dollars for new Colorbond roof. Additional costs of two thousand to five thousand dollars for disposal, battens, and incidentals. Grand total typically ranges from twenty-five thousand to thirty-five thousand dollars, including GST. Cost per square meter usually falls between one hundred forty and one hundred ninety dollars.

## Workflow 3: Emergency Storm Repair

Emergency storm repair projects require immediate response and different pricing structures. These workflows prioritize rapid assessment, temporary weatherproofing, and follow-up permanent repairs.

### Project Characteristics

Emergency storm repairs typically involve roof damage from severe weather events requiring immediate attention. The affected area usually ranges from twenty to one hundred square meters, though damage assessment may reveal larger issues. These projects demand immediate response (within hours), temporary weatherproofing to prevent further damage, insurance documentation and coordination, and follow-up permanent repairs. Initial response occurs within twenty-four hours, with temporary repairs completed in one to three days and permanent repairs scheduled based on insurance approval and material availability.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

**Step 1: Emergency Call and Initial Response**

When you receive an emergency call, gather critical information immediately. Determine the nature and extent of damage (blown-off sheets, punctures, structural damage), assess whether the property is secure or actively leaking, confirm the property address and best access point, obtain the client's contact number and availability, and check if this is an insurance claim (affects documentation requirements).

Provide immediate guidance to the client on temporary measures they can take, such as placing buckets under leaks, covering exposed areas with tarps if safe to do so, moving valuables away from leak areas, and taking photographs of damage for insurance purposes. Advise them not to attempt roof access themselves due to safety risks.

Dispatch your Emergency Response crew or make arrangements to attend personally. Confirm the estimated arrival time with the client and provide your contact number for any urgent updates.

**Step 2: On-Site Assessment and Temporary Repairs**

Upon arrival, conduct a rapid but thorough safety assessment. Check for structural stability before roof access, identify electrical hazards from damaged wiring or equipment, assess slip and fall risks from water or debris, and evaluate weather conditions for safe working. Document the damage extensively through photographs from multiple angles, videos showing the extent of damage and water ingress, measurements of affected areas, and notes on the cause of damage (wind, hail, falling debris).

Implement immediate temporary weatherproofing by installing tarps over exposed areas, securing loose or damaged sheets, blocking water entry points, and protecting the interior from further water damage. Ensure all temporary measures are secure and safe.

Provide the client with an initial verbal assessment of the damage extent, estimated cost for temporary repairs, timeline for permanent repairs, and insurance claim process guidance.

**Step 3: Creating the Emergency Project**

Create the project in Venturr immediately, even from your mobile device if necessary. Use a clear title such as "Emergency Storm Repair - [Address] - [Date]." Set the property type to Residential or Commercial as appropriate. Input client contact information and mark the project as "Emergency" or "Urgent" in the status field.

Document all work performed during the emergency response, including arrival time and departure time, temporary measures implemented, materials used for temporary repairs, and labor hours for emergency response. This documentation is critical for insurance claims and client billing.

**Step 4: Detailed Damage Assessment and Permanent Repair Planning**

Once the immediate emergency is addressed, conduct a detailed assessment for permanent repairs. Measure the affected roof area accurately and assess whether surrounding areas were weakened by the storm. Check for hidden damage to battens, sarking, or structure. Evaluate whether partial repair is sufficient or if larger sections need replacement. Consider whether this is an opportunity to recommend upgrading to more durable materials.

Determine the scope of permanent repairs, including the area requiring sheet replacement, any structural repairs needed, flashing or ridge capping replacement, gutter or downpipe repairs, and interior damage repair coordination.

**Step 5: Labor Calculator Configuration for Emergency Work**

Open the Labor Calculator and configure it for the permanent repair work. In the Dimensions tab, enter the measured area requiring repair. Select the roof type matching the existing structure. Input the roof pitch. Choose the replacement material, ideally matching the existing roof. Set waste percentage to fifteen to twenty percent for repair work, as matching and cutting increase waste.

In the Labor tab, set complexity based on the repair scope. Repairs are often more complex than new installation due to matching and integration challenges. Enter valleys and hips if the repair area includes these features. Select "Emergency Response" crew if rapid completion is required, or "Repair & Maintenance" crew for standard timeline repairs. Choose your region for cost adjustments.

Under Advanced Factors, set the material type to match the replacement material. Set removal type based on what needs to be removed for the repair. Select the season for weather delay calculations. Enable optional on-costs for comprehensive cost coverage.

In the Pricing tab, set your profit margin. Emergency work typically commands premium pricing (thirty to forty percent) due to rapid response and disruption. Enable GST inclusion.

**Step 6: Emergency Response Pricing**

For the emergency response portion of the work, calculate costs separately from permanent repairs. The Emergency Response crew includes a flat callout fee of three hundred fifty dollars. Apply time-of-day multipliers: after-hours (weeknights, early morning) at 1.5x standard rate, weekends (Saturday-Sunday) at 2.0x standard rate, and public holidays at 2.5x standard rate.

Calculate the emergency response labor cost by multiplying hours worked during emergency response by the crew hourly rate, then by the appropriate time multiplier, and adding the callout fee. Add materials used for temporary repairs, typically tarps, fasteners, and sealants.

**Step 7: Comprehensive Quote Generation**

Navigate to the Quote Generator and create a detailed quote with clear sections. Begin with an Emergency Response section itemizing the callout fee, emergency labor hours with applicable multipliers, temporary weatherproofing materials, and the subtotal for emergency work.

Follow with a Permanent Repairs section detailing roof sheet replacement for the affected area, structural repairs if needed, flashing and ridge capping replacement, color matching and integration work, and the subtotal for permanent repairs.

Include an Insurance Documentation section noting that detailed documentation is provided for insurance claims, photographs and damage assessment reports are included, and you can liaise directly with insurance assessors if needed.

Set payment terms appropriate for emergency work: emergency response costs are typically due immediately or within seven days, and permanent repair costs follow standard terms (deposit and balance on completion). Include terms covering insurance claim processing, potential additional damage discovery, and warranty on repair work.

**Step 8: Insurance Coordination and Project Completion**

If this is an insurance claim, provide the client with comprehensive documentation including detailed photographs and videos of damage, written damage assessment report, itemized quote for permanent repairs, and timeline for repair completion. Offer to communicate directly with the insurance assessor and attend any site inspections with the assessor.

Once permanent repairs are approved, schedule the work promptly. Use the Emergency Response or Repair & Maintenance crew for rapid completion. Complete the permanent repairs according to the approved scope. Conduct a final inspection with the client and provide warranty documentation for the repair work.

### Key Settings for This Workflow

Configure the calculator for emergency repairs with these specific settings: Complexity is often higher than the area suggests due to integration challenges. Material type should match the existing roof for consistency. Removal type should reflect what needs to be removed for the repair. Crew selection should be Emergency Response for urgent work or Repair & Maintenance for standard timeline. Season is less relevant for emergency work, as repairs proceed regardless of weather. Time multipliers must be applied for after-hours, weekend, or holiday work. The callout fee of three hundred fifty dollars must be included for emergency response.

### Expected Results

A typical emergency storm repair affecting fifty square meters should yield these results: Emergency response duration of two to four hours for initial assessment and temporary repairs. Permanent repair duration of one to three days depending on damage extent. Emergency response cost of one thousand to two thousand dollars, including callout fee and premium rates. Permanent repair labor cost of two thousand to four thousand dollars. Materials cost of one thousand five hundred to three thousand dollars. Grand total typically ranges from five thousand to ten thousand dollars, including GST. Cost per square meter is higher than new construction (one hundred fifty to two hundred dollars per square meter) due to integration complexity and premium rates.

## Workflow 4: Commercial Warehouse

Commercial projects require different crew configurations, safety protocols, and project management approaches compared to residential work. This workflow covers large-scale commercial roofing projects.

### Project Characteristics

Commercial warehouse projects typically involve large roof areas ranging from eight hundred to five thousand square meters or more. These projects feature low-pitch or flat roofs, simple rectangular designs with minimal complexity, and commercial-grade materials such as Klip-Lok or similar profiles. They require enhanced safety protocols and documentation, coordination with facility operations and other trades, and staged completion to minimize business disruption. Timelines typically range from three to twelve weeks depending on the project size and complexity.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

**Step 1: Commercial Client Engagement and Site Assessment**

When engaging with commercial clients, the process differs significantly from residential work. Schedule a comprehensive site meeting with key stakeholders including the facility manager, project manager, and safety officer. Discuss business operations and how roofing work will impact them, including operating hours and access restrictions, areas that must remain operational during work, noise and disruption limitations, and safety requirements for working above occupied spaces.

Conduct a detailed site assessment focusing on commercial-specific factors. Measure the roof area accurately, as commercial buildings often have complex layouts. Assess the roof structure and load-bearing capacity for crew and materials. Identify access points for materials, equipment, and personnel. Note any rooftop equipment such as HVAC units, solar panels, or skylights. Evaluate safety requirements including fall protection, edge protection, and exclusion zones. Document existing roof condition and any structural concerns.

Review any architectural or engineering plans provided by the client. Verify measurements against actual site conditions and identify any discrepancies or concerns that need engineering review.

**Step 2: Project Setup and Documentation**

Create the commercial project in Venturr with a detailed title such as "Commercial Warehouse - [Company Name] - [Address]." Set the property type to Commercial and input complete client information, including multiple contacts for different aspects of the project (facility manager, accounts payable, safety officer). Set the project status to "Estimating" and add tags for commercial, warehouse, or other relevant categories.

Prepare preliminary project documentation including a site safety plan, traffic management plan if required, environmental management plan for waste and runoff, and communication plan for client updates. These documents demonstrate professionalism and may be required for tender submissions.

**Step 3: Labor Calculator Configuration for Commercial Projects**

Open the Labor Calculator and configure it for the commercial project scale. In the Dimensions tab, enter the measured roof dimensions, which may be very large for warehouse projects. Select Flat or Gable as the roof type, as most warehouses have simple roof forms. Input the roof pitch, typically low (five to fifteen degrees) for commercial buildings. Choose commercial-grade roofing material such as Klip-Lok, Trimdek, or custom profiles. Set waste percentage to ten to twelve percent for large, simple commercial roofs.

In the Labor tab, set roof complexity, typically Simple or Standard for warehouse buildings unless there are significant penetrations or equipment. Enter valleys and hips, usually minimal for warehouse roofs. Select "Commercial Crew" or "Commercial Large-Scale" crew depending on project size. For projects over eight hundred square meters, the Commercial Crew (one supervisor plus four qualified plus one apprentice) is appropriate. For projects over fifteen hundred square meters, the Commercial Large-Scale crew (two supervisors plus six qualified plus two apprentices) provides optimal efficiency. Choose your region for cost adjustments.

Under Advanced Factors, set the material type to Colorbond or Metal for commercial installations. Set removal type if this is a re-roofing project, though many commercial projects are new construction or complete replacements. Select the season for weather delay calculations, noting that commercial projects often have fixed deadlines that may require weather contingencies. Enable optional on-costs, which are essential for commercial project accuracy.

In the Pricing tab, set your profit margin for commercial work, typically fifteen to twenty-five percent depending on project complexity and competition. Enable GST inclusion, as all commercial quotes include GST.

**Step 4: Calculate and Analyze Commercial Project Results**

Click "Calculate Takeoff" and review the results with a commercial perspective. For a twelve hundred square meter warehouse, examine the installation hours, typically two hundred fifty to four hundred hours depending on complexity. Note the crew efficiency multiplier effect, as the Commercial Crew provides 2.5x efficiency and Commercial Large-Scale provides 3.5x efficiency compared to a standard residential crew. Review the total days required, accounting for the larger crew size and efficiency. Check the weather delay buffer, which may need adjustment for projects with fixed deadlines. Analyze the labor cost per square meter, typically lower for large commercial projects due to economies of scale.

Examine the cost breakdown carefully. Materials cost for commercial projects is substantial, often forty thousand to eighty thousand dollars for a twelve hundred square meter roof. Labor cost reflects the larger crew but shorter timeline, typically fifteen thousand to thirty thousand dollars. The grand total for a commercial warehouse project of this size typically ranges from seventy thousand to one hundred thirty thousand dollars, including GST. Cost per square meter for commercial projects is typically lower than residential, ranging from sixty to one hundred ten dollars per square meter due to simpler design and economies of scale.

Review the crew breakdown to understand the commercial crew structure. The Commercial Crew includes one supervisor providing oversight and quality control, four qualified tradespeople for efficient installation, and one apprentice for material handling and support. The Commercial Large-Scale crew adds a second supervisor for dual oversight, two additional qualified tradespeople for faster completion, and a second apprentice for larger material volumes.

**Step 5: Additional Commercial Project Considerations**

Beyond the calculator results, commercial projects require additional cost items and considerations. Enhanced safety measures include comprehensive fall protection systems, edge protection and barriers, safety signage and exclusion zones, and daily safety briefings and documentation. These safety measures typically add three to five percent to the project cost.

Traffic management may be required if the project affects vehicle or pedestrian access, including traffic control plans, signage and barriers, and coordination with local authorities. Project management and coordination costs should be included for large commercial projects, typically five to ten percent of the project value, covering daily progress reporting, coordination with other trades, client communication and meetings, and quality assurance documentation.

Insurance and compliance requirements for commercial work include increased public liability insurance coverage, commercial-grade workers compensation, compliance with commercial building codes and standards, and engineering certification if required. Testing and commissioning may be required for commercial roofs, including water testing for leak detection, thermal imaging for insulation verification, and final inspection and certification.

Add these items to your quote as separate line items with clear descriptions and justifications.

**Step 6: Generate Professional Commercial Quote**

Navigate to the Quote Generator and import the labor calculation. Structure the quote professionally for commercial clients with clear sections. Begin with an Executive Summary providing project overview, total cost, timeline, and key benefits.

Detail the Scope of Work section with comprehensive descriptions of roof installation including area, material specifications, and installation methods. Include safety measures and compliance with relevant standards. Describe project management and coordination processes. Outline quality assurance and testing procedures. Specify warranty terms and conditions.

Create a detailed Cost Breakdown section with line items for materials (itemized by component), labor (broken down by phase if staged), safety and compliance measures, project management and coordination, testing and commissioning, and contingencies for weather or unforeseen conditions. Provide a subtotal, GST, and grand total.

Specify the Project Timeline with a detailed schedule showing mobilization and setup, roof installation phases (if staged), testing and commissioning, and demobilization and cleanup. Include milestones for client review and approval points.

Outline Payment Terms appropriate for commercial projects, typically structured as a deposit of twenty to thirty percent on contract signing, progress payments at defined milestones (thirty to forty percent), and final payment of twenty to thirty percent on practical completion. Include retention clauses if standard for commercial work in your region.

Provide comprehensive Terms and Conditions covering safety and compliance requirements, site access and working hours, coordination with other trades, variation and change order processes, weather delay provisions, warranty terms and exclusions, and dispute resolution procedures.

Include supporting documentation such as company safety policies, insurance certificates, trade licenses and certifications, and references from similar commercial projects.

**Step 7: Commercial Client Presentation and Negotiation**

Present the quote to the commercial client in a formal meeting with key stakeholders present. Prepare a professional presentation covering project understanding and approach, technical solution and material specifications, safety and compliance measures, project timeline and milestones, cost breakdown and value proposition, and company experience and references.

Be prepared to answer detailed questions about safety protocols and compliance, project management and communication, quality assurance and testing, warranty terms and coverage, and payment terms and conditions. Commercial clients often negotiate pricing, timeline, or terms, so understand your margins and flexibility.

If the project is part of a tender process, ensure your submission meets all requirements and deadlines. Follow up appropriately according to the tender process guidelines.

**Step 8: Project Execution and Management**

Once the commercial project is awarded, execute it with professional project management. Conduct a pre-start meeting with the client and all stakeholders to confirm scope, timeline, safety requirements, communication protocols, and site access arrangements. Mobilize the Commercial or Commercial Large-Scale crew with all necessary equipment and safety gear.

Implement daily project management practices including morning safety briefings, progress tracking against schedule, daily communication with the client, photographic documentation of work completed, and quality checks at each stage. Conduct regular progress meetings with the client, typically weekly for large projects, to review completed work, discuss any issues or variations, confirm upcoming work, and update the timeline.

Complete the project with final inspections, testing and commissioning as specified, comprehensive documentation including as-built drawings, warranty documentation and handover, and client sign-off on practical completion.

### Key Settings for This Workflow

Configure the calculator for commercial projects with these specific settings: Complexity is typically Simple or Standard for warehouse buildings. Material type should be commercial-grade profiles like Klip-Lok or Trimdek. Removal type depends on whether this is new construction or re-roofing. Crew selection should be Commercial Crew for eight hundred to fifteen hundred square meters, or Commercial Large-Scale for larger projects. Season selection affects timeline but commercial projects often have fixed deadlines requiring contingency planning. Optional on-costs must be enabled for accurate commercial pricing. Additional items for safety, project management, and testing must be added separately.

### Expected Results

A typical twelve hundred square meter commercial warehouse project should yield these results: Total duration of eighteen to twenty-five days with the Commercial Crew, or twelve to eighteen days with the Commercial Large-Scale crew. Installation hours of three hundred to four hundred hours (base time before crew efficiency). Labor cost of fifteen thousand to thirty thousand dollars depending on crew selection and region. Materials cost of forty thousand to eighty thousand dollars for commercial-grade roofing. Additional costs of five thousand to fifteen thousand dollars for safety, project management, and testing. Grand total typically ranges from seventy thousand to one hundred thirty thousand dollars, including GST. Cost per square meter usually falls between sixty and one hundred ten dollars, lower than residential due to economies of scale.

## Workflow 5: Heritage Building Restoration

Heritage building restoration requires specialized skills, materials, and approaches. This workflow covers the unique requirements of working on heritage-listed or historically significant buildings.

### Project Characteristics

Heritage building restoration projects involve heritage-listed or historically significant buildings requiring specialized treatment. Roof areas typically range from one hundred to five hundred square meters. These projects demand custom materials and fabrication, often using copper, zinc, or traditional profiles. Heritage approval processes must be followed, along with strict compliance with conservation guidelines. Specialized tradesperson skills are essential for authentic restoration work. Timelines are extended, typically ranging from four to twelve weeks, due to precision requirements and approval processes.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

**Step 1: Heritage Assessment and Stakeholder Engagement**

Heritage projects begin with comprehensive assessment and engagement with multiple stakeholders. Schedule meetings with the property owner to understand their vision and requirements, heritage advisors or architects involved in the project, local council heritage officers, and heritage consultants if engaged. Discuss the heritage significance of the building, previous restoration work and documentation, heritage approval requirements and processes, conservation guidelines that must be followed, and budget and timeline expectations.

Conduct a detailed heritage-sensitive site assessment. Document the existing roof condition through extensive photography and detailed notes. Research the original roof design and materials using historical records, photographs, or architectural drawings. Assess the condition of original materials and whether they can be conserved or must be replaced. Identify any previous alterations or repairs that may not be heritage-appropriate. Evaluate structural condition and any hidden issues that may be revealed during work.

Engage with heritage authorities early in the process. Understand the approval requirements for the proposed work and identify any restrictions on materials, methods, or design. Determine whether heritage approvals are required before quoting or after client acceptance. Establish timelines for approval processes, which can significantly extend the project timeline.

**Step 2: Heritage Project Setup and Documentation**

Create the heritage project in Venturr with a descriptive title such as "Heritage Restoration - [Building Name] - [Address]." Set the property type to Heritage or Residential with heritage tags. Input detailed client information and add notes about heritage status and approval requirements. Set the project status to "Heritage Assessment" initially, then move to "Estimating" after approvals are understood.

Prepare comprehensive project documentation that demonstrates your understanding of heritage requirements. This includes a heritage impact statement outlining how the proposed work respects the building's significance, conservation methodology describing materials and methods that comply with heritage guidelines, photographic documentation of existing conditions, and research on original roof design and materials. This documentation may be required for heritage approvals and demonstrates professionalism to the client.

**Step 3: Material Selection and Sourcing**

Heritage projects often require specialized materials that differ from standard roofing products. Research and source appropriate materials such as custom copper or zinc roofing for high-end heritage buildings, traditional profile metal roofing that matches original designs, heritage-appropriate fasteners and fixings that are concealed or match the aesthetic, custom flashings and trims fabricated to match original details, and traditional ridge capping and finials.

Contact specialized suppliers who can provide heritage-appropriate materials. Obtain pricing, lead times, and minimum order quantities. Verify that proposed materials comply with heritage guidelines and will be acceptable to heritage authorities. Document material specifications and sources for inclusion in heritage approval applications.

**Step 4: Labor Calculator Configuration for Heritage Work**

Open the Labor Calculator and configure it for the heritage project's unique requirements. In the Dimensions tab, enter the measured roof dimensions carefully, as heritage buildings often have irregular shapes. Select the roof type, which may be complex for heritage buildings with multiple roof forms. Input the roof pitch, which can be steep or irregular on heritage buildings. Choose the roofing material, selecting custom options if available or the closest match. Set waste percentage to twenty to twenty-five percent for heritage work due to custom fabrication and fitting requirements.

In the Labor tab, set roof complexity, typically Complex or Very Complex for heritage buildings due to irregular shapes and precision requirements. Enter valleys, hips, and other features, which are often numerous on heritage buildings. Select "Heritage & Custom Work" as the crew composition. This specialized crew includes one supervisor with heritage experience and two senior tradespeople with custom fabrication skills. Choose your region for cost adjustments.

Under Advanced Factors, set the material type to match the heritage-appropriate material selected. This may be copper, zinc, or custom profiles with significantly higher labor multipliers than standard materials. Set removal type if existing roofing must be removed, noting that heritage buildings may have multiple layers of roofing from previous work. Select the season for weather delay calculations. Enable optional on-costs for comprehensive cost coverage.

In the Pricing tab, set your profit margin for heritage work, typically thirty to forty percent due to specialized skills and complexity. Enable GST inclusion.

**Step 5: Calculate and Analyze Heritage Project Results**

Click "Calculate Takeoff" and review the results with an understanding of heritage project economics. The Heritage & Custom Work crew has an efficiency multiplier of 0.7x, meaning the work takes longer than standard installation due to precision requirements. Additionally, the precision multiplier of 2.0x doubles the time for custom fabrication work. These factors significantly increase labor hours and costs compared to standard roofing projects.

For a two hundred twenty square meter heritage building, examine the installation hours, which may be two hundred to four hundred hours due to the reduced efficiency and precision requirements. Review the total days required, typically twenty-five to forty days for heritage work. Check the weather delay buffer, which adds additional time. Analyze the labor cost per square meter, which is significantly higher for heritage work, often one hundred fifty to two hundred fifty dollars per square meter for labor alone.

Examine the cost breakdown carefully. Materials cost for heritage projects is substantial, often twenty thousand to sixty thousand dollars for custom copper, zinc, or traditional profiles. Labor cost reflects the specialized crew and extended timeline, typically thirty thousand to fifty thousand dollars. The grand total for a heritage restoration project of this size typically ranges from seventy thousand to one hundred fifty thousand dollars, including GST. Cost per square meter for heritage projects is significantly higher than standard residential, ranging from three hundred to six hundred dollars per square meter due to custom materials and specialized labor.

Review the crew breakdown to understand the Heritage & Custom Work crew structure. This crew includes one supervisor with heritage restoration experience providing oversight and quality control, and two senior tradespeople with custom fabrication skills for precise, authentic work. The smaller crew size reflects the precision nature of the work rather than speed of completion.

**Step 6: Additional Heritage Project Considerations**

Beyond the calculator results, heritage projects require additional cost items and considerations. Heritage approval costs include fees for heritage applications, consultant fees for heritage impact statements or conservation plans, and engineering certification if required for structural work. These costs typically range from two thousand to ten thousand dollars depending on the complexity of approvals required.

Custom fabrication work often exceeds the calculator's precision multiplier. Additional custom fabrication may be needed for decorative ridge capping and finials, custom valleys and flashings to match original details, hand-formed gutters or downpipes, and ornamental metalwork or features. Price these items separately based on the complexity and time required.

Conservation of original materials may be possible and desirable for some elements. This includes careful removal and storage of salvageable original materials, restoration or repair of original elements where possible, and reintegration of conserved original materials into the restored roof. Conservation work requires specialized skills and should be priced separately.

Documentation and reporting requirements for heritage projects include photographic documentation of all stages of work, detailed records of materials and methods used, as-built drawings showing the completed work, and final reports for heritage authorities and the client. Include time and costs for this documentation in your quote.

**Step 7: Generate Comprehensive Heritage Quote**

Navigate to the Quote Generator and import the labor calculation. Structure the quote to address heritage-specific requirements and demonstrate your understanding of conservation principles. Begin with a Heritage Project Overview describing the building's heritage significance, proposed restoration approach and methodology, compliance with heritage guidelines and approvals, and expected outcomes and benefits of the restoration.

Detail the Scope of Work section with comprehensive descriptions of heritage assessment and documentation, heritage approval processes and timelines, custom material sourcing and fabrication, roof restoration including area, materials, and methods, conservation of original elements where possible, and quality assurance and heritage compliance verification.

Create a detailed Cost Breakdown section with line items for heritage approvals and consultants, custom materials (itemized by component with specifications), specialized labor (broken down by phase), custom fabrication work, conservation of original elements, documentation and reporting, and contingencies for heritage-related discoveries or requirements. Provide a subtotal, GST, and grand total.

Specify the Project Timeline with a detailed schedule showing heritage approval processes (which can take weeks or months), material sourcing and custom fabrication lead times, roof restoration phases, conservation work, and documentation and final reporting. Include milestones for heritage authority inspections or approvals during the work.

Outline Payment Terms appropriate for heritage projects, typically structured as a deposit of thirty to forty percent on contract signing (to cover heritage approvals and material deposits), progress payments at defined milestones (thirty to forty percent), and final payment of twenty to thirty percent on completion and heritage authority sign-off. Include provisions for variations if heritage authorities require changes during the work.

Provide comprehensive Terms and Conditions covering heritage compliance and approval requirements, conservation methodology and principles, custom material lead times and specifications, variation processes for heritage-related discoveries, documentation and reporting requirements, warranty terms appropriate for heritage work, and dispute resolution procedures.

Include supporting documentation such as company experience with heritage projects, references from similar heritage restorations, trade qualifications and heritage training, and preliminary heritage impact assessment or conservation methodology.

**Step 8: Heritage Client Presentation and Approval Process**

Present the quote to the heritage client in a detailed meeting, as heritage projects require significant explanation and discussion. Prepare a professional presentation covering the building's heritage significance and your understanding of it, proposed restoration approach and methodology, custom materials and their appropriateness, specialized crew skills and experience, heritage approval processes and timelines, cost breakdown and value proposition, and company experience with heritage projects.

Be prepared to answer detailed questions about heritage compliance and approval processes, conservation methodology and principles, custom material specifications and sourcing, specialized tradesperson qualifications and experience, project timeline including approval processes, and warranty terms for heritage work. Heritage clients are often deeply invested in the building's history and will appreciate detailed, thoughtful responses.

Work collaboratively with heritage architects, consultants, and authorities throughout the approval process. Provide technical input on materials and methods. Attend heritage authority meetings or site inspections as required. Be flexible and responsive to heritage authority requirements, which may necessitate quote adjustments.

**Step 9: Heritage Project Execution and Documentation**

Once the heritage project is approved and awarded, execute it with meticulous attention to heritage requirements. Conduct a pre-start meeting with the client, heritage consultants, and any heritage authority representatives to confirm scope, methodology, approval conditions, documentation requirements, and communication protocols.

Mobilize the Heritage & Custom Work crew with all necessary specialized tools and equipment. Implement careful project management practices including daily photographic documentation of all work, detailed records of materials and methods used, regular communication with the client and heritage consultants, conservation of any original materials encountered, and quality checks against heritage guidelines at each stage.

Conduct regular progress meetings with the client and heritage consultants to review completed work against heritage guidelines, discuss any discoveries or issues, confirm upcoming work, and update the timeline. Be prepared for heritage authority site inspections during the work and ensure all work complies with approval conditions.

Complete the project with final inspections by the client, heritage consultants, and heritage authorities. Provide comprehensive documentation including photographic records of all stages of work, detailed material specifications and sources, as-built drawings showing the completed work, conservation reports for any original materials, and final reports for heritage authorities. Obtain client sign-off and heritage authority approval of the completed work. Provide warranty documentation appropriate for heritage restoration work.

## Best Practices

Across all project types, certain best practices ensure successful outcomes and client satisfaction. These practices apply universally, though their specific application varies by project type.

### Measurement and Assessment

Accurate measurement is the foundation of accurate estimation. Always conduct site visits when possible rather than relying solely on plans, as actual conditions often differ from drawings. Use laser measures for accuracy and safety, and take comprehensive photographs to document conditions and assist with later planning. For complex roofs, break them into simple sections and measure each section separately, then sum the areas. Account for overhangs and eaves in your measurements, and verify measurements against plans if available to identify any discrepancies.

Assess complexity honestly and conservatively. It is better to overestimate complexity slightly and deliver under budget than to underestimate and lose money. Count valleys, hips, and penetrations carefully, as each adds installation time. Consider access challenges that may affect crew efficiency, such as steep driveways, narrow side access, or high-traffic areas. Note any structural concerns that may require additional work or engineering input.

### Crew Selection

Choosing the appropriate crew for each project type is critical for profitability and client satisfaction. Match crew size to project scope and timeline requirements. Larger crews complete work faster but cost more per hour, while smaller crews cost less per hour but take longer to complete the work. Consider the client's priorities: if timeline is critical, select a larger, more efficient crew; if cost is the primary concern, a smaller crew may be appropriate.

Use specialized crews for specialized work. The Re-Roofing Specialist crew is significantly more efficient for projects involving removal. The Heritage & Custom Work crew is essential for heritage projects and will deliver better outcomes than a standard crew. The Emergency Response crew is appropriate for urgent work requiring immediate response. Don't use specialized crews for standard work, as their premium rates are not justified for routine projects.

Consider crew efficiency multipliers when estimating timelines. The calculator accounts for these automatically, but understanding them helps you explain timelines to clients. A Commercial Large-Scale crew with 3.5x efficiency will complete work in less than one-third the time of a standard crew, which justifies the higher hourly cost for large projects.

### Regional and Seasonal Considerations

Regional cost-of-living adjustments are significant and should always be applied. Sydney and Gold Coast have the highest adjustments at fifteen percent and twelve percent respectively, reflecting the higher cost of living and labor rates in these markets. Regional areas may have baseline or even negative adjustments, reflecting lower costs but also potentially lower market rates. Verify that your regional adjustment aligns with your actual costs and market rates.

Seasonal weather delay buffers are essential for realistic project timelines. Winter projects in particular require significant buffers, as rain, cold, and shorter days all impact productivity. Communicate these buffers to clients clearly, explaining that they represent realistic timelines rather than pessimistic estimates. Consider offering seasonal discounts for summer work when weather delays are minimal, as this can help balance your workload across the year.

Be aware of regional differences in WorkCover rates, which significantly impact labor costs. NSW has the highest rate at 10.81 percent for roofing services, while Queensland's rate is estimated at 4.5 percent. These differences affect your competitiveness in different markets and should be factored into your pricing strategy.

### Material Selection and Specification

Choose materials appropriate for the project type and client requirements. Standard Colorbond is suitable for most residential projects and offers good value. Premium materials like Klip-Lok or custom profiles are appropriate for commercial projects or high-end residential work. Heritage projects require custom materials that match original designs, regardless of cost.

Specify materials completely in your quotes, including product name, BMT thickness, color, and any special environmental specifications. This level of detail builds credibility with clients and ensures there are no misunderstandings about what is being supplied. For coastal areas, specify marine-grade fasteners and appropriate corrosion protection.

Understand material labor multipliers and how they affect project costs and timelines. Tile roofs take two to three times longer to install than metal roofs, which must be reflected in your pricing. Slate is even slower and requires highly skilled tradespeople. Communicate these differences to clients when discussing material options, helping them understand the cost implications of their choices.

### Pricing and Profitability

Set profit margins appropriate for project complexity and risk. Standard residential work typically commands twenty to twenty-five percent profit margins. Complex work such as re-roofing, heritage restoration, or emergency response can justify thirty to forty percent margins due to higher complexity and risk. Commercial work may have lower margins (fifteen to twenty-five percent) due to competitive tendering, but larger project values can still deliver good profitability.

Always include all mandatory on-costs in your pricing: superannuation at twelve percent, WorkCover at the appropriate state rate, public liability insurance at 2.5 percent, and PPE and safety equipment at two percent. These are real costs that must be covered. Consider including optional on-costs (tools, vehicles, administration) for more accurate total cost estimation, particularly for larger projects where these costs are significant.

Price additional work items separately with clear descriptions. Removal, disposal, structural repairs, and other additional work should be itemized separately from the main roof installation. This transparency helps clients understand where their money is going and justifies your total price.

### Communication and Documentation

Communicate clearly and professionally with clients throughout the process. Provide realistic timelines that account for weather delays and other factors. Explain your pricing structure and how you calculate costs, using the calculator's detailed breakdowns to demonstrate transparency. Address client concerns promptly and thoroughly.

Document everything comprehensively. Take photographs before, during, and after the work. Keep detailed records of materials used, hours worked, and any issues encountered. For insurance work, emergency repairs, or heritage projects, documentation is particularly critical. Provide clients with comprehensive documentation at project completion, including warranty information, material specifications, and maintenance recommendations.

Follow up with clients after project completion to ensure satisfaction and address any concerns. Request testimonials or reviews from satisfied clients, as these are valuable for winning future work. Maintain relationships with past clients for future maintenance work and referrals.

### Continuous Improvement

Review your estimates against actual project outcomes to improve accuracy over time. Track actual hours worked, materials used, and costs incurred for each project. Compare these to your estimates and identify any consistent variances. Adjust your estimating approach based on this feedback to improve accuracy.

Stay current with industry developments including material innovations, installation techniques, regulatory changes, and market rates. Update your pricing and methods regularly to reflect current conditions. Invest in training for yourself and your crew to maintain high skill levels and efficiency.

Use the Venturr platform's features fully to maximize efficiency and accuracy. Explore all calculator options and settings to ensure you're using the most appropriate configurations for each project type. Leverage the quote generator to create professional, consistent quotes. Utilize project management features to track progress and maintain organization.
